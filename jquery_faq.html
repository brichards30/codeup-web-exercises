National Parks FAQs

What government agency oversees the National Park Service?

The National Park Service is a bureau of the Department of the Interior. Directly overseeing its operation is the department's Assistant Secretary for Fish, Wildlife and Parks.

How many employees are in the National Park Service?

Permanent, temporary, and seasonal employees: Approximately 20,000. Volunteers: over 200,000.

When was the National Park System created?

The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916.

What is the most-visited national park?

Blue Ridge Parkway.

How many people visit the national parks?

In 2019, 327,516,619.

How many areas are in the National Park System?

The system includes 423 areas covering more than 85 million acres in every state, the District of Columbia, American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands.
